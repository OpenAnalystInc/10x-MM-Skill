# The 11 Commandments of Great Landing Page Copy

> **Used by**: Copywriting Agent
> **When**: Writing body copy, CTAs, and validating all written content

---

## The 11 Commandments

### 1. Remove as much text as possible
Every word must earn its place. If removing it doesn't hurt, remove it.

### 2. Write the strongest true statement that you can
Don't hedge. "Might help" → "Helps". "Could save" → "Saves".

### 3. What sells in-person sells online
The same objections, proofs, and emotional hooks that work face-to-face work in copy.

### 4. It's not about you; it's about what's in it for them
Transform every sentence:
- "We built..." → "You get..."
- "Our technology..." → "This means you can..."

### 5. Cleverness is the enemy, clarity is the antidote
If you have to choose between clever and clear, choose clear. Always.

### 6. Write like someone you'd want to buy from
Clear, confident, honest, and (if brand appropriate) funny.

### 7. Write for the person on the fence
They're interested but skeptical. Address their doubts directly.

### 8. Write melodically
Vary sentence length. Short sentences punch. Longer sentences provide rhythm and flow that keeps readers engaged.

### 9. Write for scannability
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max)
- Bold key phrases
- Bullet points for lists
- Clear section headers

### 10. Write for bookiness
The subheads alone should tell 90% of the story. Someone scanning just the headers should understand your value.

### 11. Get them nodding about the problem, then show the solution
Agitate the pain first. Make them think "yes, that's exactly my problem" before revealing your solution.

## CTA Copy Guidelines

### Expose the Value
Make the value visible and close-at-hand:
- ❌ "Sign up" → ✅ "Start your 14-day free trial"
- ❌ "Subscribe" → ✅ "Start learning French"
- ❌ "Submit" → ✅ "Reserve My Free Spot"

### Address Objections in CTA
- Time objection: "Get started in 60 seconds"
- Money objection: "Start free, no credit card"
- Trust objection: "Try risk-free for 30 days"

### Don't Make Users Dread Clicking
- ❌ "Sign up for emails"
- ✅ Something they'd actually want to click

## The 7 Slide Types (Page Sections)

### 1. Benefits
What outcomes/results do users get?

### 2. Features
What does the product actually do?

### 3. Teach by Example
Show the product in action solving real problems.

### 4. Address Objections
Counter the top 3 reasons they might not buy.

### 5. Secondary Calls-to-Action
For those not ready to convert (learn more, see demo).

### 6. Social Proof
Testimonials, logos, numbers, reviews.

### 7. Final Call-to-Action
The closing push with urgency/reassurance.

---

## Social Proof Types (Ranked by Power)

1. **Specific results with numbers** (strongest)
2. **Video testimonials**
3. **Well-known brand logos**
4. **Written testimonials with photo/name**
5. **Customer count**
6. **Press mentions**
7. **Awards**
8. **Years in business** (weakest)

---

## Copy Quality Checklist

- [ ] Every claim could be true (no overpromising)
- [ ] All 3 objections addressed somewhere
- [ ] User-focused (you > we)
- [ ] Brand voice consistent throughout
- [ ] Scannable format
- [ ] Subheads tell the story alone
- [ ] CTA matches conversion goal
- [ ] No jargon or buzzwords
